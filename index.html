About Me

My name is Dung Le, a Computer Science Enthusiast/ Major at University of Rochester. My areas of interest are Parallel/ Distributed Systems, Web development, and Algorithms. Check out my blog for more quality contents.

In 2020, I had a chance to represent University of Rochester and Northeast Region of USA to compete in ICPC North America Championships in Georgia Tech. My team ranked 24th out of 500 university teams nationally.

In my opinion, to tackle big problems, one must factor complexity into pieces that can fit in someone's brain, and the key to such factoring is to create abstractions that hide complexity behind a simple mental model. This technique can be applied to problems in fine-grained multi-threading to massive distributed systems.
